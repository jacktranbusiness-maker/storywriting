## 011 — Wealthy Patient Berates Orderly → He Donated Liver Lobe to Child in This Ward

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category E #2
- **Opening style**: Dialogue slap

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Boston Mass General ICU, mid-afternoon. Wealthy patient Theodore Whitcomb berates ICU orderly Carlos Mendez for being "too slow" and bringing the wrong-size cup. "Get me someone competent. I am paying for this room."
- **Clue**: Carlos doesn't argue, says "yes, sir," takes the cup back. As he turns, his scrubs ride up exposing a thin pale surgical scar on his right side. Charge nurse Margaret Hayes sees it and stops mid-stride.
- **Turn**: Margaret pulls up Theodore's family chart on her tablet, walks back, and says evenly: "Mr. Whitcomb, the orderly you just dismissed donated thirty percent of his liver to your niece in this exact ward two years ago."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Carlos was a directed-but-anonymous donor for Theodore's then-eight-year-old niece Lily who had biliary atresia. The family was told only "anonymous, hospital-affiliated." Theodore had never noticed Carlos before today. He apologizes. He tries to send a check. Carlos refuses. Carlos asks instead that Theodore fund the hospital's living-donor support program.

**Character Lock**
- Carlos Mendez — 32, dark hair short, blue ICU scrubs over white tee, beat-up gray sneakers, hospital ID badge clipped to chest, faint surgical scar visible at right hip when scrubs ride up
- Theodore Whitcomb — 60s, silver hair combed back, navy blazer over hospital gown, IV taped to left hand, gold watch on right wrist, expression of irritation
- Margaret Hayes — 40s, brown hair pulled into ponytail, ICU charge-nurse navy scrubs, stethoscope around neck, tablet in hand
- Setting — Boston Mass General ICU private room, mid-afternoon, fluorescent overhead lighting, monitors at bedside, blue curtain divider half-drawn, IV pole, vase of fresh flowers on side table
- Object clue — Carlos's surgical scar / Theodore's family chart on Margaret's tablet (pays off in full story Act 3–4)

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Theodore propped up in hospital bed, finger pointed at Carlos, mid-rant. Carlos turning to leave with wrong-size cup in hand, scrubs riding up at his right hip exposing the thin pale surgical scar. Margaret at bedside, tablet half-raised, looking from the scar to Theodore.

## 012 — Team Chaplain Cut → Star Quarterback Stands Up

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category G #5
- **Opening style**: Visual reveal

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Friday morning quarterly contract review at Pittsburgh Steelers headquarters. Team president Donald Greer reads off chaplain contract: "Pastor Henry Watts, twenty-two years, $48k stipend. Recommendation: non-renewal. He doesn't generate ROI." Two front-office execs nod.
- **Clue**: Star quarterback Marcus Stone, who walked in late from morning workout, stands up from the back of the room. He doesn't speak. He just stands.
- **Turn**: Marcus says quietly: "Sir, before you sign that. Pastor Watts is the reason I played in last year's championship. I called him at two in the morning the night before. He drove four hours from his daughter's bedside. His daughter died that morning. He preached the team's pre-game devotional anyway because he made me a promise eleven years ago. If you cut him, you cut me."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Pastor Watts has buried two players, married seven, baptized fourteen children of staff. Marcus's own sister died at age 9 of leukemia in 2013; Pastor Watts drove six hours to be at her bedside. Donald scratches the non-renewal. The stipend doubles. Marcus quietly funds half of it himself going forward.

**Character Lock**
- Marcus Stone — 29, dark close-cut hair, navy team-logo polo over white tee, athletic gray pants, Super Bowl ring on right hand, leather notebook in left hand, posture of calm restraint
- Donald Greer — 55, silver hair combed back, charcoal suit with team-logo lapel pin, gold pinkie ring, reading glasses pushed up on forehead, contract folder on desk
- Two front-office execs — late 40s, gray suits, sitting flanking Donald, both holding pens above contract pages
- Setting — Pittsburgh Steelers front-office conference room, Friday morning, gray overcast light through floor-to-ceiling windows looking onto practice field, dark wood conference table, team logo etched into back wall
- Object clue — Marcus's leather notebook (a private ledger of Pastor Watts's quiet acts)

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Marcus standing quietly behind his chair at the back of the room, leather notebook in left hand, polo damp at the collar from morning workout. Donald mid-pen-stroke about to sign non-renewal contract. The two execs caught between watching Donald and watching Marcus.

## 013 — DMV Clerk Mocks Immigrant Woman → Daughter Reveals She's a Physician

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category K #4
- **Opening style**: Object clue

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Tuesday morning at Hackensack NJ DMV. Clerk Cheryl Ruiz loudly tells fifty-something Mrs. Linh Tran for the third time that the form is in English and she has to fill it out herself. "Ma'am, this is the United States. We use English here. There are eleven people behind you."
- **Clue**: Mrs. Tran's adult daughter Hue Nguyen rushes in from work, places a hand on her mother's shoulder, and starts to translate quietly in Vietnamese.
- **Turn**: Hue finishes translating. Then in clear English to Cheryl: "She would like the medical examiner card form for her commercial driver's license renewal." Pause. "She is a physician. She delivered three thousand babies across three countries before she came here. She has been a U.S. citizen for fourteen years. She is filling out the wrong form because someone at this counter handed her the wrong one twenty minutes ago."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Mrs. Tran is a board-certified obstetrician who completed U.S. re-licensing in 2017, runs a low-income women's health clinic in Paterson NJ four days a week, and was at the DMV today only to renew the commercial license needed for the mobile clinic van that she drives herself on the fifth day. The supervisor, who has been listening, walks over, processes the correct form in seven minutes, and asks Cheryl to step into her office.

**Character Lock**
- Mrs. Linh Tran — 56, silver hair in low neat bun, gold-rimmed glasses, pale yellow blouse over dark slacks, simple gold wedding band, leather purse on counter, calm posture
- Hue Nguyen (daughter) — 28, dark hair pulled back, soft blue work blouse, dark jeans, laptop bag on shoulder, hand on her mother's right shoulder
- Cheryl Ruiz — 38, brown hair pulled tight in ponytail, blue DMV polo with name badge, expression of impatience, finger pointed at form
- Setting — New Jersey DMV office (Hackensack), Tuesday mid-morning, fluorescent overhead lighting, scuffed linoleum floor, queue of eleven people visible behind in line, plastic counter with bullet-resistant glass
- Object clue — Mrs. Tran's medical examiner card form (currently the wrong form on counter) / Hue's small careful translations in margins

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Cheryl leaning across the counter mid-sentence, finger pointing at the form. Mrs. Tran sitting straight, hands folded in lap on counter, gaze steady. Hue half-leaning on counter, mouth opening to speak, hand on her mother's shoulder. Queue of eleven in soft focus behind.

## 014 — Fine Dining Turns Away Old Man → Businessman Recognizes His Shelter Benefactor

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category A #2
- **Opening style**: Silent witness

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Wednesday evening 7:42 PM at upscale reservation-only Manhattan restaurant. Maître d' Sebastian Cole firmly directs an elderly man in a worn brown overcoat back toward the door: "I'm sorry, sir, this establishment is reservation-only and the dress code is strictly enforced." The old man (Charles Whitfield, 78) does not argue.
- **Clue**: Across the dining room, businessman Paul Henderson half-rises from his banquette. He saw the door open. He saw the battered fedora. His fork stops mid-motion.
- **Turn**: Paul stands fully. Walks across the dining room — past three other tables — to the front. Stops behind the maître d'. Voice steady but loud enough to carry: "Sebastian. Step aside, please. This man has a reservation. With me. He's just early."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Charles ran the Bowery Mission shelter where Paul slept for four months in 1987 after his first business failed. Charles gave him a hot meal, a clean shirt, the use of a payphone, and the bus fare to a job interview. Paul never told Charles his name; Paul has not seen Charles for thirty-eight years. He came in tonight because he had read in a Times obituary that morning that the original Bowery Mission director had retired to a small studio on the Lower East Side and was "looking for somewhere quiet to have a hot meal." Paul has been looking for Charles for thirty-eight years.

**Character Lock**
- Charles Whitfield — 78, gray hair under battered brown felt fedora, weathered face, brown wool overcoat with thrift-store collar over a faded blazer and dark tie, scuffed brown leather shoes, calm posture, hands at his sides
- Sebastian Cole — 40, slick black hair, black two-piece suit with lapel pin, white shirt, clip-on bowtie, polished oxfords, leather reservation book under arm
- Paul Henderson — 52, salt-and-pepper hair, charcoal three-piece suit, light-blue silk tie, silver wedding band, expression of dawning recognition
- Elena Henderson — 50s, silver hair in elegant chignon, cream silk dress, pearl earrings, half-risen from banquette
- Setting — reservation-only upscale Manhattan restaurant near Lincoln Center, Wednesday evening 7:42 PM, low warm pendant lights, dark wood paneling, white linen tablecloths, scattered cocktail-attire diners in soft focus, brass-mounted reservation podium at front
- Object clue — Charles's battered brown felt fedora / Paul's silent recognition

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Sebastian standing at the podium with one arm gently extended toward the door, body angled to direct Charles back out. Charles standing patiently, no anger, hat in hand. Paul mid-stride from his banquette across the dining room, expression urgent. Other diners turning to look.

## 015 — DA's Retirement Speech → Sister of Wrongfully Convicted Stands Up

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category H #1
- **Opening style**: Accuser POV

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Thursday evening 6:30 PM, retirement ceremony for District Attorney Robert Gantry at the Hillsborough County Courthouse great hall, Tampa. He has just said, "...thirty-six years of service to this county, and not one conviction overturned on my watch."
- **Clue**: A woman in the third row — Diane Velasquez — stands up. She does not interrupt yet. She just stands, holding a thin manila folder.
- **Turn**: Diane raises her voice just enough to carry past the podium: "Mr. Gantry. May I speak. My brother Miguel served eleven years and four months in Raiford. Your office knew he didn't do it. I have the memo." She walks slowly forward. Two security guards start to move. The clerk of court Frances stops both with one raised hand.
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Diane is herself a public defender. Miguel served 2008–2019; the Innocence Project secured his release in 2019. The internal memo Diane is holding — obtained via FOIA after a two-year legal battle — is dated July 2009, from the lead prosecutor to Gantry's then-deputy, flagging that the eyewitness ID had been corrupted and the case should not proceed. Gantry was personally cc'd. He proceeded anyway. Diane has waited for this exact retirement ceremony for six years to deliver the memo on the record before he could vanish into a pension.

**Character Lock**
- Diane Velasquez — 44, dark hair pulled back into low chignon, black professional knee-length dress, simple gold cross necklace, thin manila folder in left hand, no visible emotion, posture of long preparation
- Robert Gantry — 66, silver hair combed back, gray three-piece suit, gold pocket-watch chain across vest, gold-rimmed glasses on chain, behind a podium with the county seal, mid-speech with hand raised
- Frances Holloway (clerk of court) — 60s, silver hair short, navy blazer, black slacks, raised hand stopping the security guards
- Setting — Hillsborough County Courthouse great hall, Thursday evening 6:30 PM, warm overhead lighting, US flag and Florida state flag flanking the podium, ~150 attendees in folding chairs in cocktail attire, polished marble floor, ceremonial podium with brass plaque
- Object clue — Diane's manila folder containing the 2009 memo

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Diane standing in third row, manila folder in left hand, voice mid-sentence projected toward the podium. Gantry behind podium with one hand still raised mid-speech, mouth open, expression caught between startled and trying to recover authority. Two security guards mid-step from the side aisle, paused by Frances's raised hand from the front row.

## 016 — Police Chief Dismisses Homeless Man → "You Were Wrapped in My Jacket"

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category C #5
- **Opening style**: Moral choice

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Monday morning at Detroit Police Department's 8th Precinct lobby. Newly promoted Chief Daniel Brooks walks briskly through lobby on the way to a press conference, waves off elderly homeless man Wilbur "Wil" Hayes who has been waiting in a lobby chair: "Sir, the desk sergeant will help you. I'm late."
- **Clue**: Wil does not move from the chair. He just looks up at Daniel, very calmly, and says: "Daniel Brooks, born 1977 at Henry Ford Hospital. Mother Karen. Father unknown."
- **Turn**: Daniel stops mid-stride. He turns. He walks back to the chair. He crouches down — ruining the press-conference creases on his uniform pants — and looks Wil in the face. Wil says, very softly: "I was the off-duty patrolman who found you on the steps of St. Mary's that February night. You were three months old. You were wrapped in my jacket. I drove you to the hospital. I went back to find your mother. I did not find her. I have been looking for her for forty-seven years."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Wil was a 24-year-old Detroit patrolman who, off-duty in 1977, found three-month-old Daniel abandoned on the steps of St. Mary's Catholic Church in a Detroit February cold snap. Wil wrapped the baby in his department-issue jacket and drove him to Henry Ford. The records were sealed under Michigan adoption law. Three weeks ago, after a state law change, Daniel — now Chief — quietly requested the unsealing. Wil read the unsealing notice in the Detroit News yesterday and walked four miles to the precinct this morning.

**Character Lock**
- Wilbur "Wil" Hayes — 71, gray beard trimmed close, threadbare navy-blue down parka over flannel, dark gray knit cap, dark jeans, scuffed brown work boots, wooden cane leaning against chair, calm pale gray eyes
- Chief Daniel Brooks — 48, dark hair short, navy police chief's uniform with brass star on collar, four chevrons on sleeve, polished black duty boots, leather portfolio under arm, peaked cap in hand
- Setting — Detroit 8th Precinct lobby, Monday morning, fluorescent overhead lighting, scuffed tile floor, public-information posters on wall (kept blurred), reception desk in background with sergeant in soft focus, row of plastic-and-vinyl chairs along one wall
- Object clue — Wil's wooden cane / records-unsealing notice in Wil's parka pocket

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Daniel mid-stride toward exit door, hand half-raised in dismissive wave back at the lobby chairs. Wil seated in third chair from end with cane against the chair, looking up calmly, mouth just opening to say the date and birth details. Reception sergeant in background looking up from her desk.

## 017 — Hungry Intern Shares Lunch with Security Guard → Founder Reveals Final Interview

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category B #1
- **Opening style**: Wrong apology

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Tuesday lunchtime at the Innova Systems cafeteria, Mountain View CA. Twenty-two-year-old intern Priya Shah eats a peanut-butter sandwich she made herself because cafeteria food is too expensive on her stipend. At the next table, a tired-looking security guard sits alone reading a paperback. He has not eaten yet.
- **Clue**: Priya hesitates, then walks over and slides her unwrapped second sandwich onto the guard's table. "I made two. I never eat both. Please." She walks back to her table without looking up. The guard looks up, smiles, says thank you, eats it slowly. Priya does not see him slip a small flesh-toned earpiece out of his ear and tuck it into his uniform pocket.
- **Turn**: At 4:55 PM the same afternoon, Priya is called into the executive boardroom for the "final round" of her internship interview. She walks in. The CEO chair is empty. A man in a charcoal suit is standing at the window. He turns around. It is the security guard from lunch.
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: The "guard" is Innova founder/CEO Marcus Webb (54), who personally observes one finalist intern per cohort by spending a lunch hour in a borrowed uniform. Sixteen of the previous sixteen interns who shared food with him were offered full-time positions; all sixteen are still at the company. Priya is offered a junior PM role at 40% above standard. She takes it. She also asks Marcus when she can repay the sandwich. He laughs and says "next Tuesday, same table, my treat" — and means it.

**Character Lock**
- Priya Shah — 22, dark hair pulled into a low ponytail, navy interview blazer over a white blouse, modest gray slacks, beat-up leather messenger bag, lanyard with photo badge that reads INTERN, simple stud earrings
- Marcus Webb — 54, salt-and-pepper hair short and tousled, gray security-guard uniform (poly-blend short-sleeve shirt with sewn-on logo, gray slacks, scuffed black work boots), holstered handheld radio, dog-eared paperback in hand, expression of mild fatigue
- Setting — Innova Systems main-campus cafeteria, Mountain View CA, Tuesday 12:14 PM, bright daylight through floor-to-ceiling windows looking onto a courtyard with a fountain, light-wood communal tables, modest queue at salad bar in background, Priya and Marcus at adjacent two-tops near the window
- Object clue — Priya's brown-paper-wrapped second sandwich / the small flesh-toned earpiece Marcus pockets

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Priya mid-step away from Marcus's table, having just slid the second sandwich onto it without making eye contact. Marcus seated, paperback open, looking up at the sandwich, expression softening, right hand just leaving his ear and dropping into his uniform pocket. Cafeteria patrons in soft background, no one paying attention.

We don't have budget for catering for non-instructional staff. He'll get a card." Mrs. Loretta Banks (31 years at the school) goes very still.
- **Clue**: At 4:02 PM, school board chair Dr. Eleanor Pace walks unannounced into the principal's office. She had heard about the cancellation from Mrs. Banks within fifteen minutes. She closes the door. She sits down.
- **Turn**: Eleanor opens a manila folder. She slides a typed list across Greg's desk. Fourteen names. "Mr. Manning. The man whose retirement reception you just canceled is Mr. Wendell Carter. He has worked at this school for twenty-two years. The names on this list are fourteen students whose graduation fees, prom tickets, and senior portrait expenses were quietly paid for, in cash, by Mr. Carter. We do not announce this. Greg uncancels the reception, attends in person, and personally writes a $500 check from his own first-month paycheck to the budget line.

- **Setup**: Wednesday morning 9:47 AM at Milwaukee County Department of Health and Human Services. - **Clue**: Tasha does not argue. She just adjusts Aaliyah on her hip and says, very evenly, "I missed appointments because I work two jobs and my daughter's daycare closed in June. The paperwork is late because the address you have on file is wrong; I sent in the change form in April."
- **Turn**: Tasha glances at the small framed family photograph on Patricia's desk — late 1990s, two adults and three children at a backyard barbecue. Tasha's eyes stop on the youngest child, a Black baby girl on the lap of a younger Patricia. Tasha asks, very quietly: "Mrs. Hall. Where was that picture taken."

Tasha grew up calling Patricia "Aunt Patty" until age 8, when the families had a falling-out. Tasha has been carrying a 23andMe result in her hoodie pocket — DNA showed a 50% match to "P. Hall" in the Milwaukee area — and was going to ask Patricia today, before Patricia opened the file.

**Character Lock**

- Setting — Milwaukee County DHHS office, Wednesday morning 9:47 AM, fluorescent overhead lighting, scuffed industrial carpet, small framed family photograph on desk facing Patricia (visible at angle to Tasha), county-seal poster on wall (kept blurred), file folders stacked on desk corner
- Object clue — small framed family photograph from late 1990s on Patricia's desk / 23andMe printout in Tasha's hoodie pocket

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Patricia leaning back in her chair with file open in her lap, mid-sigh, reading glasses sliding down her nose, head half-shaking. Tasha seated across with Aaliyah on her hip, eyes locked on the small framed family photograph at the edge of Patricia's desk. Aaliyah's small hand on Tasha's hoodie collar.

## 020 — Judge Dismisses Home Cook's "Simple" Dish → Mexican Judge Recognizes Family Recipe

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category I #4
- **Opening style**: Room goes wrong

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Saturday afternoon 2:14 PM, regional finals of the Southwest Home Cook Championship at the Phoenix Convention Center. Lead judge Vincent Marquand has just tasted home cook Lucia Mendez's small earthenware bowl of mole negro. He puts down his spoon and says into the live broadcast camera: "It's — well, it's a stew. Unsophisticated. Provincial. I taste no technique."
- **Clue**: The second judge — Dr. Rosa Delgado, half-Mexican-American culinary historian and James Beard Foundation board member — does not put down her spoon. She takes a second taste. Then a third. Her free hand goes very slowly to her chest.
- **Turn**: Rosa, on the live broadcast, sets the spoon down with deliberate care, looks across the tasting station at Vincent, and says: "Vincent. May I taste it once more before you continue." She takes a fourth taste. She closes her eyes. She opens them, looks at Lucia, and asks very quietly: "Where did you learn this. Specifically. Which village."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Lucia's grandmother was from a small Zapotec village outside Oaxaca that produced a regional variation of mole negro using forty-three ingredients including a specific mountain chile that grows in only three valleys. Rosa had spent six months researching this exact mole for a James Beard regional-heritage report in 2003; the version Lucia made tonight is the first time in twenty years Rosa has tasted it outside that specific village. Lucia wins the championship by unanimous vote (with Vincent quietly recusing on grounds of "additional context required"). Rosa flies Lucia to a James Beard ceremony three months later as a regional-heritage honoree.

**Character Lock**
- Lucia Mendez — 38, dark hair pulled back into a smooth low bun, simple white kitchen apron over a teal blouse, dark slacks, brown leather flats, small silver hoop earrings, her grandmother's small wooden cooking spoon tucked into apron pocket
- Vincent Marquand — 57, salt-and-pepper hair combed back, navy double-breasted chef coat with embroidered French-and-American credentials over a black shirt, blue jeans, polished black shoes, expression of dismissive boredom
- Dr. Rosa Delgado — 48, dark hair in low neat chignon, navy blazer over white blouse, James Beard Foundation lanyard, simple gold studs, tasting spoon in right hand, expression shifting from polite to focused recognition
- Setting — Phoenix Convention Center main competition hall, Saturday afternoon 2:14 PM, bright overhead stage lighting, three-judge tasting station with white linen runner, small earthenware bowl of dark mole negro between them with garnish, broadcast camera on a jib visible in background, audience in scattered banked seating in soft focus
- Object clue — small earthenware bowl of mole negro / Lucia's grandmother's small wooden spoon in her apron pocket

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Vincent leaning back in his chair with arms crossed, mid-dismissive sentence into the broadcast camera. Lucia standing very still on the cook's side of the tasting station, her grandmother's wooden spoon visible in her apron pocket, hands folded neatly. Rosa seated between them, leaning forward, spoon paused above the earthenware bowl, taking her fourth taste, free hand against her chest.
