## 001 — Waitress Returns Wallet → Missing Daughter

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category A #6
- **Opening style**: Object clue

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Late-night Brooklyn diner. Wealthy patron Robert Hale loses his wallet. Waitress Mia Carter returns it. Robert's wife Vivian publicly accuses Mia of theft and inspects every bill in front of the booth.
- **Clue**: As Vivian counts cash, Mia glimpses a faded 2001 photograph tucked behind the credit cards — a young woman holding a baby wearing a silver locket identical to Mia's.
- **Turn**: Robert notices Mia's frozen stare. He slides the photograph out of the wallet and looks from the photo to Mia's face. The eyes match.
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Mia is the baby in the photograph. Robert's late sister gave her up for adoption 24 years ago. The locket was pinned to the baby's blanket. The Hale family has been searching ever since. Vivian's accusation collapses; Robert kneels.

**Character Lock**
- Mia Carter — 24, brown hair pulled into a low ponytail, navy diner apron over white tee, simple silver locket at the collarbone
- Robert Hale — 60s, salt-and-pepper hair, charcoal three-piece suit, no tie, brown leather wallet open in his hand
- Vivian Hale — 50s, blond chignon, cream cashmere coat, pearl earrings, accusatory finger
- Setting — Brooklyn corner diner booth, late night, warm yellow pendant lights, chrome counter behind, half-empty coffee cups on Formica table
- Object clue — faded 2001 photograph in plastic sleeve behind credit cards

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Vivian leans across the booth, finger pointing at Mia, mid-accusation. Mia stands still holding the open wallet, eyes locked on the photograph she just glimpsed. Robert sits between them, head tilted toward the photo, half-aware that something is wrong.

## 002 — Officer Removes Vet → K9 Refuses to Move

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category C #2
- **Opening style**: Silent witness

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Crisp October afternoon, Austin city park. Officer Tyler Brooks (28) approaches a bench where Frank Donnelly (68) is eating a sandwich from a paper bag. "Sir, you can't sleep here. Let's go."
- **Clue**: Tyler's K9 partner Ranger freezes mid-step, ears forward. He breaks heel and lies down at Frank's boots, head pressed against Frank's right ankle. Frank doesn't move; he just watches the dog.
- **Turn**: Tyler tugs the leash. Ranger doesn't budge. Frank slowly opens his field jacket, reaches into the inside pocket, and brings out a worn leather case — military ID, a unit patch, a 2003 photograph of a younger Frank kneeling beside a black-and-tan K9 with the same markings.
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Frank trained the lineage Ranger came from. He was the lead K9 trainer for the unit until it was decommissioned in 2008. He lost his marriage to PTSD and ended up here. Ranger recognizes the scent of the trainer who shaped his bloodline. Tyler radios in; the precinct identifies Frank.

**Character Lock**
- Frank Donnelly — 68, gray beard trimmed close, faded olive Army field jacket with subdued unit patch over flannel, dark blue jeans, scuffed brown work boots
- Officer Tyler Brooks — 28, blond buzz cut, navy police uniform with shoulder patch, leather duty belt with K9 leash clipped, polished tactical boots
- Ranger — adult German Shepherd, black and tan, police K9 vest with handle
- Setting — Austin neighborhood park, early afternoon, low golden October light, oak trees in background, painted-wood bench, scattered fallen leaves
- Object clue — leather military ID case in Frank's inside coat pocket (visible mid-reach)

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Officer Brooks has one hand on the leash, other gesturing at Frank to stand and leave. Ranger is lying pressed against Frank's right boot, head down on his paws. Frank's hand is half-into his jacket pocket, eyes calm and locked on the officer.

## 003 — Coach Cuts Smallest Kid → Pro Scout Walks Down

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category F #6
- **Opening style**: Visual reveal

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Friday night JV basketball tryout cuts in a suburban Cincinnati high school gym. Coach Mark Reilly reads names from his clipboard. "Davis, Cole, Henderson — you're cut." He waves Eli Henderson (16, 5'7", #14) toward the door without looking up.
- **Clue**: A man in the upper bleachers — gray quarter-zip pullover, lanyard around his neck, notebook in his lap — closes the notebook and starts down the aluminum steps. The clack of his footsteps draws every parent's attention.
- **Turn**: The scout (Daniel Cho, 50s, lanyard reads "Pacers Scouting / Pro Player Development") reaches the gym floor and stops in front of Coach Reilly. Loud enough for the room: "I drove three hours to watch number 14 specifically. He's got the fastest 40-time I've recorded all season."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Daniel has been tracking Eli through summer AAU film. Eli's frame is small but his footwork is elite. The high school staff didn't film tryouts. Eli skips JV entirely and is invited to a regional development camp. Coach Reilly is suspended for evaluation negligence.

**Character Lock**
- Eli Henderson — 16, 5'7", lean build, brown hair damp with sweat, white practice jersey #14, black athletic shorts, low-cut basketball sneakers, gym bag at feet
- Coach Mark Reilly — 45, balding crown, navy school-logo polo, khakis, whistle on lanyard, clipboard in left hand
- Daniel Cho — 50s, salt-and-pepper hair, gray quarter-zip pullover with small embroidered Pacers logo, dark jeans, black notebook, professional lanyard with photo badge
- Setting — high school gymnasium, Friday night, fluorescent overhead lights reflecting off polished maple floor, scoreboard at 0-0, mostly empty bleachers except scattered parents in mid-rows, basketball rack against side wall
- Object clue — scout's notebook with handwritten timing splits

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Coach Reilly mid-sentence, dismissive hand gesture toward the gym exit. Eli stands frozen, gym bag at his feet, looking down. In the upper bleachers behind them a man in a gray pullover is descending the aluminum steps, drawing every parent's gaze — but the coach hasn't looked up yet.

## 004 — Lawyer Mocks Elderly Witness → Photograph on the Table

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category H #4
- **Opening style**: Accuser POV

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Brightly lit deposition room, downtown Atlanta civil case. Defense attorney Brian Mathers leans across the polished table at Eleanor Whitman (72) and says: "With respect, Mrs. Whitman — you were two hundred yards away at dusk. Memory at your age is not testimony."
- **Clue**: Eleanor doesn't argue. She slowly opens her brown leather purse and withdraws a worn photograph in a clear plastic sleeve. She places it face-up on the table between them. The court reporter's hands pause mid-keystroke.
- **Turn**: The photograph shows the defendant standing at a Texaco station 47 miles from the alleged crime scene. Timestamp 8:47 PM. Date stamped. Signed across the bottom: "Witnessed — H. Whitman, owner, 9/14/22."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Eleanor and her late husband owned the photo lab that ran the gas station's surveillance archive. She has been a forensic photographer for 51 years. Her husband signed off on every timestamp before he died. The defendant's alibi has been verifiable for 18 months — the prosecution buried it. The case ends within ten minutes.

**Character Lock**
- Eleanor Whitman — 72, silver hair pulled into a low bun, gold-rimmed glasses on a beaded chain, lilac knit cardigan over cream blouse, single strand of pearls, brown leather purse on lap
- Brian Mathers — 40s, dark hair gelled back, navy three-piece suit, burgundy silk tie, gold pinkie ring, polished oxfords, thick legal pad with notes
- Setting — courthouse deposition room, mid-morning, fluorescent overhead with sun through tall windows, polished mahogany table, court reporter's stenotype to the side, water pitcher and glasses
- Object clue — timestamped photograph in clear plastic sleeve

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Brian leans forward with both palms flat on the table mid-accusation, finger pointed at Eleanor. Eleanor sits straight, her hand just settling the plastic-sleeved photograph onto the wood between them. Court reporter's hands paused mid-keystroke, eyes flicking to the photo.

## 005 — CEO Humiliates Employee → Assistant Whispers Surgery

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category B #5
- **Opening style**: Dialogue slap

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Glass-walled boardroom in a Manhattan financial firm, late afternoon. CEO Howard Vance stands at the head of the table tearing into Marcus Reed in front of the entire deal team over a Q3 miss: "If this is the best you can do, maybe try a job that pays in compliments."
- **Clue**: Howard's executive assistant Jenna Lee steps quietly to his side, tablet in hand, and whispers something into his ear. His face changes mid-sentence. The team holds its breath.
- **Turn**: Jenna's whisper is short: "Mr. Vance — Marcus is the donor who paid for your daughter's surgery last spring. He used his entire bonus. Mrs. Vance knows. He never told you."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Howard's daughter is alive because Marcus's anonymous $187,000 donation paid for the experimental treatment Howard's insurance refused. Howard's wife has been protecting the secret to spare Marcus's pride. Howard sits down. The room watches a man rebuild his face. Marcus is named co-lead on the next deal that afternoon.

**Character Lock**
- Marcus Reed — 35, brown hair short, navy off-the-rack blazer over white shirt, modest red tie, dark slacks, leather laptop bag at feet
- Howard Vance — 55, silver hair combed back, charcoal three-piece suit, light-blue silk tie, gold cufflinks, wedding ring
- Jenna Lee — 40s, black hair in chignon, cream blouse, charcoal pencil skirt, slim tablet, silent posture
- Setting — Manhattan high-rise glass-walled conference room, late afternoon, sunset light through floor-to-ceiling windows, polished walnut conference table, six other team members frozen in seats, deal book printouts and water bottles
- Object clue — Jenna's tablet (donor records open on screen, hidden at her side)

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Howard mid-rant, leaning forward over the table, finger pointed at Marcus. Marcus sits still, hands flat in his lap, eyes on the wood. Jenna at Howard's right shoulder, leaning to his ear with the tablet held discreetly at her side. Other team members visible in soft focus, avoiding eye contact.

## 006 — Cold Ring Return → Diagnosis Note

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category J #5
- **Opening style**: Wrong apology

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Quiet Italian restaurant, Boston North End, Friday night. James Ortiz (32) places the engagement ring back on the white tablecloth in front of his fiancée Amelia Park (29) and says, "I'm sorry. I can't do this anymore." He doesn't look up. His hand is shaking.
- **Clue**: Amelia's sister Olivia (27, sitting one table over with her husband — was meant to "casually witness" the surprise anniversary toast) catches the tremor. She quickly pulls a small folded note from her clutch and slides it across the floor to Amelia's foot.
- **Turn**: Amelia reads the note: "He found out two days ago. Stage 4. He doesn't want you to stay out of pity. STOP HIM."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: James was diagnosed with stage-4 pancreatic cancer 48 hours earlier. He believes ending things now spares Amelia from caregiving and grief. Amelia catches him in the rain outside. She doesn't ask for the ring back. She tells him she's already decided. They sit on the curb. The story ends with a different conversation.

**Character Lock**
- Amelia Park — 29, dark hair in soft waves to shoulders, deep emerald silk slip dress, simple gold hoop earrings, manicured nude nails
- James Ortiz — 32, black hair short, dark navy suit jacket open over white shirt, no tie, hands visibly shaking
- Olivia Park (sister) — 27, similar features to Amelia, burgundy wrap dress, small silver clutch, seated at adjacent two-top
- Setting — small Italian restaurant in Boston's North End, candlelight, warm amber wall sconces, white tablecloth, single rose in glass vase, half-finished glasses of red wine, exposed-brick wall behind
- Object clue — open ring box on white tablecloth between them; folded note in Olivia's hand

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: James slides the open ring box back across the tablecloth toward Amelia, hand mid-retreat, eyes on the table. Amelia sits frozen, lips slightly parted, hand near her chest. Olivia at the adjacent table half-turned, sliding a folded note toward Amelia under the lip of the tablecloth.

## 007 — Funeral Director Pushes Out Homeless Man → Letter on Casket

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category D #7
- **Opening style**: Moral choice

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Modest funeral parlor, Cleveland OH, late morning service. Funeral director Charles Briggs sees a disheveled man (Walter "Wally" Kasprak, 60s) hovering near the open casket. Mourners are upset. Charles approaches: "Sir, you need to move along. This service is family only."
- **Clue**: Wally doesn't argue. He reaches into the inside pocket of his threadbare overcoat and brings out a folded letter on yellowed legal paper. He carefully places it on the polished wood of the casket beside the framed photograph of the deceased in dress uniform.
- **Turn**: The widow Helen steps forward, picks up the letter — and her hand goes to her mouth. The handwriting is her late husband's. Dated three weeks before his death. Addressed: "Walter, my brother by every definition that matters."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Wally and the deceased served together in the Marines, 1991. After Wally's PTSD ended his marriage and put him on the street, the deceased had been quietly paying his rent for 22 years through a small family trust. Helen knew. The letter is a final goodbye and a thank-you for "keeping me alive in 1991." Charles offers Wally a chair in the front row.

**Character Lock**
- Walter "Wally" Kasprak — 60s, weathered face, gray stubble, navy knit cap, threadbare olive wool overcoat over plaid flannel and stained tee, worn denim, scuffed brown work boots
- Charles Briggs — 50s, black suit, white shirt, black tie, polished black shoes, name badge, clipboard tucked under arm
- Helen (widow) — 60s, navy mourning dress, black hat with veil, pearl earrings, in front pew
- Setting — small Cleveland funeral parlor, mid-morning, warm wall sconces, polished wood casket with white floral spray, framed photo of deceased in Marine dress blues on stand, mourners in dark suits and dresses in pews
- Object clue — folded yellowed legal-paper letter

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Wally stands beside the casket, his hand just settling the folded letter onto the polished wood next to the framed photograph. Charles strides toward him from frame-left, hand gesturing for him to leave. Several mourners in pews behind turning to look.

## 008 — Michelin Inspector Snubs Busboy → Chef Runs from Kitchen

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category I #2
- **Opening style**: Room goes wrong

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Upscale Manhattan restaurant, Saturday night service. A Michelin inspector (Daniel Ashford, 50s, charcoal sport coat, observing posture) finishes his entrée and waves off elderly busboy Mr. Tien Nguyen (78) reaching to clear the plate: "Don't bother. Send the waiter."
- **Clue**: Mr. Tien withdraws his hand, gives a polite half-bow, and steps back. From the kitchen pass, executive chef Annie Cole (40, white chef coat) sees the exchange and pushes through the swinging door at full pace.
- **Turn**: Annie reaches the table, places her hand gently on Mr. Tien's shoulder, and addresses the inspector loud enough for the next two tables to hear: "Sir, please. He is the reason this restaurant exists. My grandfather learned to cook from him in Saigon in 1972. He clears plates because he insists on staying useful."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Mr. Tien is the original chef whose techniques the restaurant is built around. He emigrated in 1975, helped Annie's grandfather start his first New York kitchen, refused ownership when offered, and works two nights a week as a busboy "to keep his hands moving." The Michelin inspector returns the next week. The restaurant earns its first star. The plaque at the door is updated with Mr. Tien's name above Annie's grandfather's.

**Character Lock**
- Mr. Tien Nguyen — 78, silver hair neatly combed, kind weathered face, white short-sleeve busboy jacket over black slacks, white busboy apron, soft canvas shoes
- Daniel Ashford — 50s, salt-and-pepper hair, charcoal sport coat over open-collar white shirt, dark trousers, leather notebook beside cleared dinner plate, water glass
- Annie Cole — 40, brown hair tied back, white double-breasted chef coat with name embroidered, navy apron, kitchen towel in hand
- Setting — upscale Manhattan dining room, Saturday evening, low warm pendant lights, dark wood booth seating, white linen tablecloth, half-finished glass of red wine, tealight candle
- Object clue — Mr. Tien's worn brass-handled bus tray (set aside on a service stand)

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Mr. Tien steps back from the table, both hands at his sides, polite half-bow. Daniel half-turns away, dismissive hand gesture, attention back on his leather notebook. Annie pushes through the swinging kitchen door behind them, mid-stride toward the table, expression urgent.

## 009 — Gallery Snubs Old Woman → She Is the Painter

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category L #1
- **Opening style**: Victim restraint

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Chelsea white-cube gallery, opening night. Gallery director Vanessa Crowe brushes off elderly Ruth Adler (76) who is studying the centerpiece painting at close range: "Ma'am, please don't lean in. The piece is insured for nine hundred thousand. The catalogue is at the front desk."
- **Clue**: Ruth doesn't move. She tilts her head, looks at the small wall plaque, and quietly asks, "Did they finally fix the attribution?"
- **Turn**: A research curator (Dr. Henry Park, 60s) crossing the gallery overhears. He sets his wine glass down and says loud enough for the room: "Vanessa. This is Ruth Adler. The attribution was corrected by our institution last winter. Her name belongs on the wall."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Ruth painted "Untitled, 1973" — credited for fifty years to her late husband, a more famous male painter, because that was the only way galleries would buy work by a woman in that era. The estate's attribution was officially corrected by Christie's the prior winter. Ruth came to see her own name in print for the first time, and Vanessa's gallery hadn't updated the plaque. The opening's marketing collapses. Ruth is given the floor.

**Character Lock**
- Ruth Adler — 76, silver hair in low bun, gold half-frame glasses, soft gray cashmere cardigan over a faintly paint-flecked cream tunic, slim navy slacks, simple wedding band
- Vanessa Crowe — 35, sharp asymmetric black bob, sleek black sheath dress, statement gold earrings, tablet in left hand, wine glass in right
- Dr. Henry Park — 60s, salt-and-pepper hair, navy blazer, white shirt open at collar, wire-rim glasses, wine glass paused mid-air
- Setting — Chelsea white-cube gallery, opening night, polished concrete floor, large abstract painting in muted earth tones on the far wall, low gallery lighting with track-light spotlights on the artwork, scattered guests in cocktail attire holding wine
- Object clue — wall plaque with old (incorrect) artist name on it

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Ruth stands close to the painting, hands clasped behind her back, head tilted in study. Vanessa is mid-gesture pointing toward the catalog desk, half-turned away, dismissive. Dr. Park visible in the background, just turning, wine glass paused mid-air, recognition dawning on his face.

## 010 — New Pastor Moves Elderly Woman → She Founded This Church

- **Concept source**: concepts1 / Category M #1
- **Opening style**: Deadline pressure

**4-beat arc**
- **Setup**: Sunday morning service, mid-sized Methodist church in Charleston SC. New pastor Reverend Thomas Hale (38) leans down to the front-row pew where Margaret Whitfield (84) sits in her Sunday best: "Sister Margaret, would you mind moving back a few rows? We have a major donor visiting today and I need this row reserved."
- **Clue**: Margaret looks up, eyes calm, but doesn't move. From two pews back, Deacon Ellis Greene (60s) stands abruptly and walks to the aisle.
- **Turn**: Deacon Greene addresses the pastor, voice low but firm enough for the front three rows to hear: "Pastor — Sister Margaret founded this congregation in 1968. In her living room. With eleven people. The land you're standing on was deeded by her family. She is the donor."
- **Payoff (NOT in photo)**: Margaret has anonymously funded the church's youth ministry, building expansion, and food pantry for 56 years. Her family deeded the land in 1971 with one condition: she always sits in the front row. The new pastor was never told. He sits down beside her, takes her hand, and apologizes. Margaret simply pats his hand and opens her hymnal.

**Character Lock**
- Margaret Whitfield — 84, silver hair under a navy felt church hat with a small silk flower, white wool tea-length coat over pale-blue Sunday dress, pearl brooch, gloved hands holding worn personal hymnal
- Reverend Thomas Hale — 38, dark hair short, navy clerical robe with white stole, thin metal-frame glasses, tablet under arm
- Deacon Ellis Greene — 60s, charcoal suit, white shirt, dark tie, gray hair close-cropped, hymnal in hand
- Setting — Charleston Methodist church sanctuary, Sunday morning, warm light through stained-glass windows in jewel tones, polished wooden pews, choir loft visible behind altar, congregation seated, organist visible at side
- Object clue — Margaret's worn personal hymnal with her late husband's name embossed in faded gold on the cover

**Peak-tension moment for photo**: Pastor Hale leans toward Margaret with an apologetic-but-firm hand gesture toward the rear pews. Margaret sits still, gloved hands folded over her hymnal, looking forward toward the altar. Deacon Greene stepping into the aisle from the row behind, hand half-raised.
