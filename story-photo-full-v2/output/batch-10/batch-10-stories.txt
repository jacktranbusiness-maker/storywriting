Even when she doesn't yet know her own grandson.

Thirty-eight years is a long time to look for someone.

But we found each other at a table full of people who were laughing.

And we got the last, best word.

Nobody shushes him anymore.

And I don't push the cart with my head down anymore either.

Because the old man in the worn coat taught me the most important thing I've ever learned about people:

You never know who you're really looking at.

So treat everyone like they built the room.
