## Per-item plans (4-beat arc · Character Lock · peak-tension photo)

### C111 — "The Legacy Kids Laughed at the Scholarship Boy in Rental Spikes → The Putt That Went Silent on 18"
- **conceptSource:** #F22-09 | **engine:** E22 Underdog Wins | **tone:** feel-good | **opening:** The return | **photo:** action-in-photo
- **Arc:** Setup: at the junior member-guest, legacy teens freeze out caddie-scholarship member Andre over his rental spikes and "charity" status. Clue: Andre quietly out-plays them all day; he reads every green like he was born on them. Turn (action-in-photo): on 18, tournament on the line, he stands over a long putt and the green goes silent. Payoff: he sinks it; reveal his grandfather caddied this course 30 years and was never allowed in the clubhouse — Andre wins with grace; the club shifts.
- **Character Lock:** Andre Wallace (17, narrator, caddie-scholarship junior member, worn rental spikes, grandfather's old blade putter); Chip Langford (17, legacy captain, pastel polo); Mr. Pruitt (60s, tournament chair). Setting: Brightwater Country Club, 18th green, Greenwich CT, late summer afternoon. Object clue: grandfather's worn blade putter.
- **Peak photo:** Andre over a long putt on 18, ball just struck and rolling; gallery of members frozen; Chip's smirk fading at frame edge. Outcome hidden.

### C112 — "I Paid Five Dollars for a Box of Old Books → What Was Tucked Inside Belonged to Someone Else"
- **conceptSource:** #F19-18 | **engine:** E07 The Honest Return | **tone:** feel-good | **opening:** Visual reveal | **photo:** action-in-photo
- **Arc:** Setup: June, broke owner of a failing used bookshop, wins a $5 lot at a charity estate auction. Clue: under the paperbacks, a cookie tin holds a handwritten unpublished manuscript + letters by a beloved late local author. Turn (action-in-photo): she lifts the manuscript into the light at the sale table. Payoff: she tracks down the author's family and returns it instead of selling — the estate insists she shepherd its publication; proceeds save her shop and fund a library reading room.
- **Character Lock:** June Okafor (38, narrator, used-bookshop owner, cardigan, reading glasses on a cord); the late author Hollis Reed; granddaughter Marian Reed (40s). Setting: charity estate auction tent, then June's shop, Asheville NC. Object clue: a tin of handwritten manuscript pages.
- **Peak photo:** June at the sale table lifting a sheaf of yellowed handwritten pages out of a battered cookie tin, eyes wide; other bidders oblivious. Twist (its value/what she does) hidden.
