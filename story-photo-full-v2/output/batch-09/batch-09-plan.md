## 4-BEAT ARCS

### C081 | "They Said the Nurse Got Nothing" → The Patent in the Will
**Engine:** E01 | **Tone:** feel-good | **Opening:** Tender moment
- **Setup:** At the reading of Arthur Pennington's will in a Hartford attorney's office, his nephew Brad and niece Sloane gloat; the hospice nurse, Grace Bennett, sits in the back, expecting nothing.
- **Clue:** The attorney sets aside a separate sealed envelope addressed to Grace; the relatives smirk that it's "probably a thank-you card."
- **Turn:** Arthur quietly assigned Grace the rights to a medical device patent he'd held for decades — the one income stream the relatives never knew existed.
- **Payoff:** Grace, who never angled for anything, becomes the steward of the patent; she funds a hospice wing in Arthur's name and keeps nursing. Kindness loops back.
- **Character Lock:** Grace Bennett — 44, hospice nurse, cardigan over scrubs, soft steady manner. Brad Pennington — 41, blazer, smug. Sloane Pennington — 38, designer bag, eye-roll. Attorney Mr. Okafor — 60, gray suit. Setting — estate attorney conference room, Hartford CT, morning. Object clue — separate sealed envelope addressed to Grace.
- **Peak-tension photo (Beat 1–2):** The relatives leaning in greedily at the conference table while Grace sits at the far end; the attorney sliding a single sealed envelope toward her, the others smirking.

### C082 | "He Hired Her to Cook for Us" → The Résumé in the Drawer
**Engine:** E00 | **Tone:** mixed | **Opening:** Dialogue slap
- **Setup:** Paul Mercer hires a live-in "personal chef," Brielle, for a trial month; his wife Diane is told it's a gift to lighten her load.
- **Clue:** Diane finds Brielle's résumé in a kitchen drawer — the listed prior home address is identical to Paul's old downtown apartment, the one he "sold years ago."
- **Turn:** The chef is the woman Paul has been seeing; the apartment was never sold — it's where they've met for two years.
- **Payoff:** Diane doesn't make a scene at dinner. She quietly consults a lawyer, secures the records (the house is in her name), and removes Paul from her life on her terms; Brielle is gone by week's end.
- **Character Lock:** Diane Mercer — 46, elegant casual, controlled expression, narrator. Paul Mercer — 49, polo, oblivious confidence. Brielle — 31, chef's apron, wary. Setting — upscale home kitchen, Atlanta GA, evening. Object clue — chef's résumé page with the telltale address.
- **Peak-tension photo (Beat 1–2):** Diane standing at the kitchen island holding a printed résumé page, Brielle at the stove glancing over, Paul mid-pour of wine, the moment before anyone says what's happening.

### C083 | "Sign Here, Ma'am, It's a Fair Price" → The Grandson with the Maps
**Engine:** E20 | **Tone:** feel-good | **Opening:** Silent witness
- **Setup:** A landman, Wade Tomlin, leans on widow Pearl Hutchins at her West Texas ranch, pushing a lowball check for her mineral rights before she "loses the chance."
- **Clue:** Pearl keeps saying she'd "like to wait for Eli"; Wade dismisses it. A dusty truck pulls up the ranch road.
- **Turn:** Eli — the grandson Pearl raised and put through school — is now a petroleum geologist; he brings survey maps showing a major formation under the fence line that Wade's company already knew about.
- **Payoff:** Pearl doesn't sell; she leases on fair terms with Eli managing it, and funds scholarships for ranch kids. The kindness she poured into her grandson returns to protect her.
- **Character Lock:** Pearl Hutchins — 71, sun-faded dress, steady hands, quiet. Eli Hutchins — 29, geologist, dusty boots, rolled maps under arm. Wade Tomlin — 52, bolo tie, lowball check. Setting — ranch porch and dusty drive, near Midland TX, dawn. Object clue — rolled geology survey maps; the lowball check on the table.
- **Peak-tension photo (Beat 1–2):** Wade leaning over the porch table sliding a check toward Pearl, who sits calm with hands folded; a dusty truck just arriving at the end of the drive in the background.

- **Peak-tension photo (Beat 1–2):** Hannah in a hospital bed holding a folded document, the Crawfords standing over the bassinet with their backs half-turned, a nurse mid-step; the quiet before the document is read.

### C089 | "We Made It, We're Approved" → The Envelope on the Floor
**Engine:** E11 | **Tone:** bittersweet | **Opening:** Letter/voicemail open
- **Setup:** Ana Solís and her husband Grant Mueller walk hand in hand into the San Antonio immigration hearing she's spent two years believing was the happy end of a real marriage.

- **Turn:** It's an agreement: Grant married Ana to satisfy a condition on a family trust, with a schedule of payments to her and a planned divorce once the trust released — the marriage was always a transaction to him.
- **Payoff (bittersweet):** The approval comes through, but the marriage Ana believed in never existed. She walks out with her status and the wreckage of two years given to a man performing love. Too late to get the years back; she chooses her own future anyway.
- **Character Lock:** Ana Solís — 29, modest dress, hopeful then hollowed, narrator. Grant Mueller — 47, suit, polished warmth. Setting — federal immigration court waiting area/hearing room, San Antonio TX, morning. Object clue — the slipped envelope and the trust agreement inside.
- **Peak-tension photo (Beat 1–2):** Ana seated on a bench in a government waiting area holding a half-opened envelope, her face just beginning to change; Grant beside her, unaware, smiling toward the hearing-room door.
