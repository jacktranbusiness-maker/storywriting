### C061 — "The Librarian Who Shushed the Man in the Worn Coat → The Name Above the Reading-Room Arch"
- **conceptSource:** concepts5 #F06-07 | **engine:** E31 Provider in Disguise | **tone:** feel-good | **opening:** Silent witness
- **Arc:**
  - Setup: Head librarian Marian publicly humiliates Walter, a shabby daily regular, threatening to pull his card for "loitering."
  - Clue: Walter never checks anything out yet knows every corner; he pauses beneath the new wing's brass plaque; a city official greets him like someone important.
  - Turn: At a board rededication, Walter is revealed as the anonymous donor who funded the entire new wing (and the staffing line).
  - Payoff: He responds with grace, not revenge — endows a page-to-librarian scholarship and asks that no one ever be shushed for needing a warm place to sit. Marian shamed but offered a chance to do better.
- **Character Lock:** Priya Nair (24, part-time page, narrator/witness); Walter Brandt (78, threadbare brown wool coat, flat cap, corner armchair); Marian Doyle (52, head librarian, tight bun, glasses on a chain). Setting: Holloway Public Library new reading wing, Dayton OH, rainy Tuesday afternoon. Object clue: brass donor plaque over the arch (unreadable in photo).
- **Peak-tension photo (Beat 1–2):** Marian leaning over Walter's armchair, finger pointed; Walter calm; Priya frozen with a book cart; plaque blurred above.
