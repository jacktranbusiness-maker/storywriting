## 4-BEAT ARCS (Summary)

### C121 | The Final Envelope → Hidden Benefactor (E20)
- **Setup:** Greedy relatives bicker through a will reading, ignoring quiet old Otis in the back.
- **Clue:** The lawyer holds one last sealed envelope and a ring of old keys.
- **Turn:** The final envelope reveals Otis — the family's former diner cook — quietly funded every grandchild's college tuition for decades.
- **Payoff:** The will leaves the diner and the boat to Otis, and a letter explaining the kindness; the family that ignored him must reckon with who actually carried them. Warm, generational.

### C122 | Smart Speaker Replay → Affair Exposed (E00)
- **Setup:** Dana asks the kitchen speaker to play the morning news.
- **Clue:** Instead, it replays a voice command Greg gave the night before — scheduling something with a name that isn't a client.
- **Turn:** The replay makes the affair undeniable, in their own kitchen, in his own voice.
- **Payoff:** Dana doesn't scream; she calmly gathers the digital trail the speaker logged and ends the marriage on her terms.

### C123 | Bell Ring → Insider Trade Exposed (E13)
- **Setup:** Trader Marcus rings the ceremonial bell; the floor cheers his record quarter.
- **Clue:** Compliance officer Aisha steps forward with a phone.
- **Turn:** She plays a recording of Marcus passing an insider tip — the lone honest voice against a celebrated star.
- **Payoff:** Marcus is walked out; Aisha, who stood alone, is vindicated when the regulators confirm she was right.

### C124 | Adoption Cake → Birth Father Returns (E11)
- **Setup:** The Brennans celebrate three years with their foster son Leo, finalization imminent.
- **Clue:** Birth father Daniel appears in the doorway with a folder of court papers.
- **Turn:** Daniel completed every requirement; his parental rights are legally restored.
- **Payoff:** Bittersweet — the Brennans must let Leo go; love and law collide and no one wins cleanly. Grace in the handoff.

### C125 | Auctioned House → 1962 Life Estate (E06)
- **Setup:** Flipper Brad wins the abandoned Victorian at auction, planning to evict and gut it.
- **Clue:** Old Eleanor stands on the porch holding a yellowed deed.
- **Turn:** A life estate recorded in 1962 gives Eleanor the legal right to live in the house until she dies — the sale can't remove her.
- **Payoff:** Brad's plan collapses; Eleanor, dismissed as a squatter, was right all along and keeps her home.

### C126 | Humble-Origins Post → Trust-Fund Stub (E12)
- **Setup:** Executive Bryce's viral "self-made from nothing" post racks up likes.
- **Clue:** Intern Naomi, who's seen the payroll, sits calmly at her laptop.
- **Turn:** Naomi posts proof — his "humble origins" salary was a trust-fund-padded executive package from day one.
- **Payoff:** The narrative collapses; comeuppance for a man who profited from a fake struggle story.

### C127 | Coach's Denial → The Recording (E13)
- **Setup:** Coach Tanner denies the abusive locker-room culture players have reported.
- **Clue:** Player Tyler holds a phone.
- **Turn:** Tyler plays the recording of the coach's tirade for the athletic director.
- **Payoff:** The lone player who refused to stay silent is believed; the coach faces consequences and the team is protected.

### C128 | Folded Flag → The Buried Soldier Lives (E11)
- **Setup:** A memorial service honors Sgt. Eli Carter, presumed killed; his mother receives the folded flag.
- **Clue:** A gaunt figure in a loose uniform stands at the back of the chapel.
- **Turn:** Eli is alive — a clerical/identity mix-up and a long captivity meant the wrong man was buried.
- **Payoff:** Bittersweet reunion — joy tangled with grief for the months lost and the family already broken by mourning.

### C129 | The Broke Regular → License Holder (E31)
- **Setup:** Room boss Dex and the table mock Sal, the rumpled regular who never seems to have money.
- **Clue:** Sal calmly slides a small laminated card onto the felt.
- **Turn:** Sal holds the room's gaming license — he quietly owns the operation Dex runs.
- **Payoff:** Sal doesn't crush Dex; he reorganizes the room to protect young players like Jamie. The "needy" man was the quiet provider.

### C130 | Cut-Off Audition → Viral Voice (E17)
- **Setup:** Choir director Reese cuts off single mom Sherise mid-audition, dismissing her.
- **Clue:** A congregant in the pews is filming.
- **Turn:** The clip of Sherise's voice goes viral; the community rallies around her.
- **Payoff:** A donor offers a music scholarship; the congregation unites for Sherise and her child — found-family warmth over gatekeeping.
