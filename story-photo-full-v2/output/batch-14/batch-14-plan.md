### C131 | "They Laughed at the Slow Old Man" → The Founder of the Wing
**Engine:** E31 | **Tone:** feel-good | **Opening:** Tender moment
- **Setup:** At a charity fun-run for the new pediatric wing, a cluster of teen volunteers mock 88-year-old Walter Lindqvist for being the slowest one on the course in his wheelchair. Teen narrator Dylan Reeves laughs along.
- **Clue:** Walter wears a faded old hospital-foundation pin; he keeps the pace anyway, finishing dead last, unbothered.
- **Turn:** At the ribbon-cutting, the hospital director introduces the anonymous founding donor who funded the entire pediatric wing decades ago and still funds the youth program the teens are in — Walter.
- **Payoff:** Walter doesn't shame the kids; he invites them to help name the playroom and quietly covers a scholarship for Dylan, who learns what he almost mocked away. Provider in disguise, grace.
- **Character Lock:** Walter Lindqvist — 88, wheelchair, cardigan, faded foundation pin, gentle. Dylan Reeves — 16, race T-shirt, teen narrator. Dr. Pham — 50, hospital director. Setting — charity run course + hospital atrium ribbon-cutting, Asheville NC, morning. Object clue — faded hospital-foundation lapel pin on Walter's cardigan.
- **Peak photo (Beat 1–2):** Teens laughing near the start line as Walter rolls slowly past in his wheelchair; the foundation pin on his cardigan catching light; the unfinished pediatric-wing banner behind.
