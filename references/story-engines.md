# Story Engines — Narrative Structure Variants

The original pipeline (concepts1/2/3) runs on ONE emotional engine: **humiliation → reveal**
(justice + surprise) — labeled **E00** below. This file defines **22 additional engines**
(E01–E22) so a feed can mix emotional drivers instead of repeating one formula.

Every engine still fits the same abstract spine the pipeline already uses:

```
HOOK  →  BUILD  →  TURN  →  PAYOFF
```

What changes per engine is only what fills those four slots — and whether an **antagonist**
exists at all. `humiliation → reveal` is simply the default instantiation:
Hook = humiliation, Turn = recognition, Payoff = antagonist consequence.

How to read each card:
- **Driver** — the emotion that earns the share (warmth / tears / awe / curiosity / catharsis…)
- **Photo (scroll-stop)** — the single moment the 4:5 photo must capture (Beat 1–2 only)
- **Withhold** — what the photo + caption must NOT reveal (the payoff)
- **Turn** — the pivot that recontextualizes everything
- **Payoff / tone** — how it lands, and the ending mood
- **Antagonist** — yes / optional / none (drives whether Act 4 needs a "consequence")
- **Opening style** — recommended caption opener(s)
- **Title** — humiliation→reveal arrow is optional; use the variant noted

---

## New neutral opening styles (add to the caption's 10)

Most of the original 10 opening styles assume someone is being judged. These 5 work for
engines with no antagonist:

| Style | Opens with… |
|-------|-------------|
| `Curious Ritual` | A strange behavior repeated over time — reader wants to know *why* |
| `Tender Oddity` | An unlikely, gentle pairing or moment that doesn't quite add up |
| `Countdown of Years` | A span of time up front ("For 31 years, every morning…") |
| `Letter Fragment` | One line lifted from a letter, note, voicemail, or inscription |
| `Quiet Watch` | Someone observing or protecting from a distance, unexplained |

## The 22 engines

> **E00 — Humiliation → Reveal (default).** The original engine used by concepts1/2/3. Tag every
> concepts1/2/3 item as `E00`. Included here so mixed-bank batches can label every item consistently.
>
> - **Driver:** justice + surprise
> - **Photo:** the humiliation/dismissal moment — antagonist blocking, mocking, or dismissing
> - **Withhold:** the protagonist's hidden identity / power / connection
> - **Turn:** recognition begins; the antagonist's certainty cracks
> - **Payoff / tone:** twist landed + antagonist consequence; satisfying
> - **Antagonist:** YES
> - **Opening style:** Dialogue Slap · Silent Witness · Accuser POV
> - **Title:** `[humiliation] → [reveal]`

### E01 — Kindness → Karma
- **Driver:** warmth + justice
- **Photo:** an unprompted act of kindness mid-moment (covering a bill, giving up a coat); outcome unknown
- **Withhold:** that the recipient can/will change the giver's life
- **Turn:** the recipient's true significance surfaces later
- **Payoff / tone:** kindness repaid; uplifting
- **Antagonist:** optional (a dismissive bystander, not required)
- **Opening style:** Moral Choice · Quiet Watch
- **Title:** `[kind act] → [unexpected return]`

### E02 — Mystery → Explanation
- **Driver:** curiosity
- **Photo:** a strange, repeated behavior or object (two coffees left on a bench, a daily detour)
- **Withhold:** the *reason why*
- **Turn:** someone follows, asks, or finally notices
- **Payoff / tone:** the explanation lands — can be warm or bittersweet
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Curious Ritual
- **Title:** `[odd ritual] → [the reason]`

### E03 — Hidden Burden (empathy reframe)
- **Driver:** empathy
- **Photo:** someone who reads as cold/distant/rigid (leaves exactly on time, never joins in)
- **Withhold:** the weight they're privately carrying
- **Turn:** someone sees where they actually go / what they actually do
- **Payoff / tone:** judgment dissolves into respect
- **Antagonist:** mild (the people who misjudged — reframed, not punished)
- **Opening style:** Quiet Watch · Accuser POV (soft)
- **Title:** `[cold behavior] → [the burden behind it]`

### E04 — The Long Wait / Devotion
- **Driver:** loyalty, longing
- **Photo:** a solitary ritual tied to a place (repainting a bench, setting one extra plate)
- **Withhold:** who/what is being waited for
- **Turn:** the meaning of the ritual is revealed
- **Payoff / tone:** poignant — fulfilled or quietly unfulfilled
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Countdown of Years · Curious Ritual
- **Title:** `[years of a ritual] → [what it was for]`


### E05 — Comeuppance (the downfall)
- **Driver:** catharsis / schadenfreude
- **Photo:** the wrongdoer at their most smug (bragging, flaunting, belittling)
- **Withhold:** that their own action is about to sink them
- **Turn:** their behavior reaches exactly the wrong witness / triggers their own ruin
- **Payoff / tone:** self-inflicted fall; satisfying
- **Antagonist:** YES — the antagonist IS the focus
- **Opening style:** Accuser POV · Dialogue Slap
- **Title:** `[arrogant act] → [self-made downfall]`

### E06 — Bittersweet / Too Late
- **Driver:** tears
- **Photo:** an ordinary moment carrying quiet dread (a son walking into a nursing home)
- **Withhold:** that it's already too late
- **Turn:** the truth arrives after the window has closed
- **Payoff / tone:** grief + love; NO happy reversal, NO antagonist consequence
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Tender Oddity · Letter Fragment
- **Title:** `[ordinary visit] → [what was already lost]`

### E07 — Test / Secret Evaluator
- **Driver:** suspense about a choice
- **Photo:** a setup that quietly tests character (a "stranded" person, a dropped wallet)
- **Withhold:** that it's a deliberate test, and who's watching
- **Turn:** the evaluator reveals themselves; the choice has consequences
- **Payoff / tone:** earned reward or earned loss
- **Antagonist:** those who fail the test (optional)
- **Opening style:** Moral Choice · Silent Witness
- **Title:** `[a small choice] → [it was a test]`

### E08 — Reunion / Recognition
- **Driver:** vỡ òa (overwhelming reunion)
- **Photo:** a clue-bearing moment between strangers (a song, a locket, a scar) — no conflict
- **Withhold:** that they are bound by the past
- **Turn:** the clue connects them
- **Payoff / tone:** reunion; cathartic, no villain
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Object Clue · Letter Fragment
- **Title:** `[a familiar detail] → [who they are]`

### E09 — Underdog Earns It
- **Driver:** satisfaction (earned, not gifted)
- **Photo:** an underestimated person about to perform under doubt
- **Withhold:** the outcome (and that it's pure skill, not hidden status)
- **Turn:** competence silences the room
- **Payoff / tone:** earned triumph — NO secret identity needed
- **Antagonist:** doubters (optional)
- **Opening style:** Deadline Pressure · Silent Witness
- **Title:** `[underestimated] → [earns it on skill]`


### E10 — Pay-It-Forward Chain
- **Driver:** hope, ripple effect
- **Photo:** one person helping another with no expectation
- **Withhold:** how far the kindness will travel
- **Turn:** the help passes through several hands and loops back
- **Payoff / tone:** warm, hopeful; no villain
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Quiet Watch · Moral Choice
- **Title:** `[one small help] → [it comes full circle]`

### E11 — Role Reversal Over Time
- **Driver:** poignancy of time
- **Photo:** a present-day moment hinting at a shared past (a patient + a young doctor)
- **Withhold:** the earlier relationship between them
- **Turn:** the past role is revealed; positions have flipped
- **Payoff / tone:** moving — the cared-for now caring, the doubted now leading
- **Antagonist:** optional
- **Opening style:** Countdown of Years · Object Clue
- **Title:** `[present role] → [the reversed past]`

### E12 — Message From the Past
- **Driver:** emotional revelation
- **Photo:** someone holding/discovering a letter, voicemail, tape, or inscription
- **Withhold:** what the message says
- **Turn:** the message reframes everything the family believed
- **Payoff / tone:** revelation; warm or aching
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Letter Fragment
- **Title:** `[a found message] → [what it changes]`

### E13 — Misunderstanding → Truth Flip
- **Driver:** relief + shame-on-behalf
- **Photo:** someone caught "in the act" of an apparent wrong (taking, hiding, leaving)
- **Withhold:** that the real action is good
- **Turn:** the innocent (often noble) truth comes out
- **Payoff / tone:** relief; accusers humbled
- **Antagonist:** the accusers (reframed, not crushed)
- **Opening style:** Accuser POV · Room Goes Wrong
- **Title:** `[apparent wrongdoing] → [the good truth]`

### E14 — Anonymous Benefactor Discovered
- **Driver:** warm surprise
- **Photo:** evidence of unexplained help (an always-paid tab, gifts with no sender)
- **Withhold:** who's behind it
- **Turn:** the secret giver is identified
- **Payoff / tone:** gratitude; tender
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Curious Ritual · Letter Fragment
- **Title:** `[secret help] → [who it was]`


### E15 — Promise Kept (years later)
- **Driver:** honor, loyalty
- **Photo:** someone arriving / working to fulfill an old vow (fixing a roof, showing up)
- **Withhold:** the promise behind the act
- **Turn:** the vow is revealed (often to someone lost)
- **Payoff / tone:** dignified, moving
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Countdown of Years · Quiet Watch
- **Title:** `[a present act] → [the old promise]`

### E16 — Redemption / Making Amends
- **Driver:** transformation, forgiveness
- **Photo:** someone returning to a place/person tied to a past mistake
- **Withhold:** the wrong they're trying to repair
- **Turn:** they actively make it right, at a cost
- **Payoff / tone:** earned forgiveness; the "antagonist" is their past self
- **Antagonist:** the protagonist's former self
- **Opening style:** Quiet Watch · Wrong Apology
- **Title:** `[a return] → [the wrong made right]`

### E17 — A Reason to Stay (the brink)
- **Driver:** hope, rescue from giving up
- **Photo:** someone at a quiet low point — about to close the shop, leave town, quit
- **Withhold:** the small sign that will pull them back
- **Turn:** an unexpected person/moment gives them a reason
- **Payoff / tone:** hope restored
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Tender Oddity · Deadline Pressure
- **Title:** `[about to give up] → [the reason to stay]`
- **⚠ Safety:** frame as quitting a dream / closing a business / leaving — NEVER self-harm imagery or ideation, in photo or text

### E18 — The Gift Comes Full Circle
- **Driver:** poetic justice / serendipity
- **Photo:** an object being given away or donated (a coat, a tool, a book)
- **Withhold:** that it will return with meaning
- **Turn:** the same object comes back, transformed by its journey
- **Payoff / tone:** poetic, satisfying
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Object Clue · Letter Fragment
- **Title:** `[a gift given] → [how it returns]`

### E19 — Quiet Protector Revealed
- **Driver:** safety, gratitude
- **Photo:** someone who seems unsettling or intrusive near another person
- **Withhold:** that they're actually shielding them from a real threat
- **Turn:** the protective truth surfaces
- **Payoff / tone:** relief + gratitude
- **Antagonist:** the real (often off-screen) threat
- **Opening style:** Quiet Watch · Accuser POV (soft)
- **Title:** `[seems threatening] → [the silent guardian]`


### E20 — The Cost of Pride
- **Driver:** tragic irony
- **Photo:** someone arrogantly rejecting / discarding something or someone "beneath" them
- **Withhold:** that they're destroying their own future
- **Turn:** time proves what they threw away was everything
- **Payoff / tone:** tragic irony — self-inflicted, NO hero rides in to rescue
- **Antagonist:** the proud person themselves (self-sabotage)
- **Opening style:** Dialogue Slap · Accuser POV
- **Title:** `[a proud rejection] → [what it cost them]`

### E21 — Found Family / Chosen Bond
- **Driver:** belonging
- **Photo:** an unlikely, warm pairing (a widower + a runaway teen, two strangers)
- **Withhold:** how the bond will form
- **Turn:** a small act binds them
- **Payoff / tone:** belonging; no blood tie, no reveal, no villain
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Tender Oddity
- **Title:** `[two strangers] → [a chosen family]`

### E22 — Reverse Rescue
- **Driver:** full-circle gratitude
- **Photo:** a crisis where one person saves another (no recognition yet)
- **Withhold:** that the rescuer was once rescued BY the person they're now saving
- **Turn:** the past rescue is revealed
- **Payoff / tone:** circular, profound; neither knew
- **Antagonist:** none
- **Opening style:** Object Clue · Countdown of Years
- **Title:** `[a rescue] → [the rescuer was once saved by them]`

## Using engines in a batch

- **Phase 0:** assign one `engine` per item (e.g. `E06`). Rotate engines across a batch so the
  feed mixes drivers — avoid 3+ consecutive items on the same engine, and don't run a whole
  batch on a single engine unless the user asks.
- **Phase 1 (photo):** build the scroll-stop from the engine's **Photo** line; obey **Withhold**.
- **Phase 2 (caption):** open with the engine's **Opening style**; the caption stops one beat
  before the **Turn**.
- **Phase 3 (story):** map Hook → Build → Turn → Payoff; only enforce an "antagonist
  consequence" when **Antagonist = yes**. Match the **ending tone**.
- Concept seeds: **E00** seeds live in `concepts1.md` / `concepts2.md` / `concepts3.md`;
  **E01–E22** seeds live in `references/concepts4.md` (grouped by engine).
- **Mixed-bank batches** (concepts3 + concepts4): see the "Mixed-bank batches" section in
  `references/batch-mode.md`, or run `node scripts/pick-batch.js` to auto-select a balanced set.
