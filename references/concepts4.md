# Concepts 4 — Engine-Based Scenario Bank (E01–E22)

Total: 660 English short-drama scenario seeds, grouped by **story engine** (30 per engine).

Unlike concepts1/2/3 (all `humiliation → reveal`), these seeds are organized by the **22 narrative
engines** in `references/story-engines.md`. Each engine has its own emotional driver, photo
scroll-stop, and ending tone — so a batch can mix engines instead of repeating one formula.

All scenarios set in the United States. Format: **Bold Title**: one-sentence seed.
When planning, read the matching engine card in `story-engines.md` for how to build the photo,
caption opening, and ending tone.

---


## E01 — Kindness → Karma

*Driver: warmth + justice. An unprompted kindness is repaid in a way the giver never expected.*

1. **The Covered Tab**: A teenage barista quietly pays for a veteran's coffee when his card declines; a month later his attorney calls — the man left her the building the café rents.
2. **The Bus Fare**: A nurse covers a stranger's bus fare without a word; years later he's the surgeon who fast-tracks her father's transplant, recognizing her face.
3. **The Last Umbrella**: A delivery driver gives his only umbrella to a soaked old woman; she turns out to own the company about to terminate his contract — and renews it instead.
4. **The Free Slice**: A pizza shop owner feeds a hungry boy every closing time for free; eighteen years later that boy buys the failing shop and keeps it open in his name.
5. **The Jumpstart**: A mechanic fixes a broken-down car at midnight refusing payment; the driver is a hospital director who later waives his daughter's entire surgery bill.
6. **The Window Seat**: A businesswoman gives up her first-class seat to a soldier's pregnant wife; the soldier's father, the airline's board chair, hears the story and rewrites their loyalty program.
7. **The Sandwich Trade**: A janitor shares his lunch with a new intern eating nothing; the intern becomes CEO and his first act is to fund a staff meal program named for the janitor.
8. **The Quarter**: A cashier slips a few coins to a kid short on milk money; that kid, now a contractor, rebuilds her flooded home for free decades later.
9. **The Rideshare Detour**: A driver detours an hour to get a stranded woman to her dying mother; she's a judge who later recuses herself but ensures his wrongful ticket is dismissed.
10. **The Warm Coat**: A homeless man gives his blanket to a shivering girl at the shelter; her family, once they find him, funds his apartment for a year.
11. **The Spare Tire**: A trucker changes a tire for a frazzled teacher in the rain; she later recognizes him at a school board meeting and saves his son's expulsion hearing.
12. **The Held Door**: A retail clerk patiently helps a confused elderly shopper everyone ignored; the shopper's son, a regional VP, promotes her over the manager who scolded her.
13. **The Paid Layaway**: A stranger anonymously pays off a struggling mom's layaway at Christmas; a year later that mom, now a loan officer, approves his small-business loan when no one else will.
14. **The Shared Charger**: A traveler lends his phone charger to a panicked man at the gate; that man, a recruiter, remembers the kindness and calls him for the job a week later.
15. **The Carried Groceries**: A boy carries an old widow's groceries up four flights every week; she leaves him the savings that put him through trade school.
16. **The Open Tab at the Diner**: A waitress feeds a down-on-his-luck musician on the house; he writes a song about her diner that goes viral and packs the place for years.
17. **The Returned Wallet**: A custodian returns a lost wallet untouched; the owner, a foundation head, creates a scholarship in the custodian's children's names.
18. **The Late-Night Tow**: A tow operator refuses payment from a crying nursing student; she becomes the ER nurse who recognizes and saves him after his crash.
19. **The Extra Plate**: A food truck owner always sets aside meals for shelter kids; the city, learning this, grants him the prime festival spot he was repeatedly denied.
20. **The Bicycle Gift**: A man gives his old bike to a kid with a long walk to work; that kid, now an engineer, designs the prosthetic that gives the man back his leg.
21. **The Patient Tutor**: A librarian tutors an immigrant teen after hours for free; the teen becomes the lawyer who later defends the library from closure.
22. **The Coffee for the Cleaner**: An office worker brings the night cleaner coffee every shift; the cleaner's daughter, an executive, ensures he's first hired when layoffs hit.
23. **The Snow Shovel**: A boy shovels an elderly couple's walk all winter expecting nothing; they fund his college the spring he thinks he can't afford it.
24. **The Front-Row Ticket**: A stagehand gives his comp ticket to a sobbing fan; she's a producer who later hires him to run her entire touring crew.
25. **The Shared Notes**: A student quietly shares her notes with a struggling classmate; he becomes the professor who later approves her tenure unanimously.
26. **The Diaper Money**: A stranger covers a young dad's diapers at checkout; that dad, now a firefighter, pulls the stranger's family from a burning duplex years later.
27. **The Kept Secret**: A pharmacist discounts a man's heart meds out of pocket; the man's grateful son, a developer, builds the pharmacy a new location rent-free.
28. **The Warm Meal for the Driver**: A family invites their shivering delivery driver in for dinner; he later becomes the inspector who quietly helps them pass a brutal home sale.
29. **The Returned Kindness Box**: A girl leaves grocery money in a stranger's mailbox; decades later the stranger, now wealthy, funds the girl's nonprofit without knowing it's her — until the reveal.
30. **The Last Seat at the Shelter**: A man gives up the last cot to a sick stranger and sleeps outside; the stranger recovers, finds him, and hires him as the shelter's new manager.


## E02 — Mystery → Explanation

*Driver: curiosity. A strange repeated behavior or object puzzles everyone — until the reason surfaces. No antagonist.*

1. **The Two Coffees**: Every morning a widow buys two coffees, drinks one, and leaves the other on a park bench before walking away — a new barista finally follows her to find out why.
2. **The Midnight Gardener**: A man waters a vacant lot's flowers at 3 a.m. every night; neighbors assume he's odd until they learn whose garden it once was.
3. **The Same Bus, Wrong Stop**: An old man rides six stops past his house every evening and walks back — a curious teen discovers the heartbreaking reason he can't go straight home.
4. **The Locked Diner Booth**: A café keeps one booth permanently reserved with a single place setting; regulars speculate for years before the owner explains.
5. **The Unsent Postcards**: A retired mailman buys a postcard from every town he visits but never sends them; his granddaughter finds the stack and the answer.
6. **The Painted Door**: A woman repaints her front door a different color every spring; the new neighbor's question unlocks a story no one on the street knew.
7. **The Empty Chair on the Porch**: An elderly man sets out two rocking chairs each dawn though he lives alone — a delivery driver finally asks.
8. **The Folded Napkins**: A diner cook folds one napkin into a crane with every order to table nine; a customer's curiosity reveals who used to sit there.
9. **The Whistling Janitor**: A school janitor whistles the same eight notes in the same hallway every day; a music teacher recognizes the tune and traces its origin.
10. **The Returned Library Book**: The same book is checked out and returned monthly, never read past page 40; the new librarian investigates and uncovers a quiet vigil.
11. **The Bench She Cleans**: A woman wipes down one specific bus bench daily, rain or shine; a commuter's question reveals what happened there.
12. **The Single Light**: A man leaves one upstairs light on every night for decades; the realtor selling the house learns why he never turned it off.
13. **The Saturday Suit**: A retiree dresses in his finest suit every Saturday and sits at the train station without boarding; a conductor finally learns who he's waiting to not-arrive.
14. **The Chalk Mark**: Someone draws a small chalk star on the same wall each morning; a shop owner stakes out the spot and discovers a touching ritual.
15. **The Extra Sandwich**: A construction worker makes two lunches but eats one; his foreman's curiosity leads to a story about a buddy he lost.
16. **The Tuesday Roses**: Fresh roses appear on a stranger's grave every Tuesday with no name; the groundskeeper's patient watch reveals the giver.
17. **The Counting Man**: A man counts steps aloud crossing the same bridge daily; a jogger learns the number matters more than anyone guessed.
18. **The Untouched Piano**: A bar keeps a piano no one's allowed to play; a traveling musician's questions finally pry loose the reason.
19. **The Morning Wave**: An old woman waves at the 7:15 freight train every day though no one waves back; a new engineer decides to find out why.
20. **The Wrapped Gift in the Window**: A toy store displays one wrapped present that's never for sale; a child's persistent asking reveals its meaning.
21. **The Backwards Clock**: A barber keeps one clock running backward; a curious regular finally gets the story behind it.
22. **The Saved Voicemail Booth**: A man visits the last working phone booth in town weekly and just listens; a documentary student uncovers what he's hearing.
23. **The Lone Swimmer**: A woman swims to the same buoy and back each dawn, then touches it twice; a lifeguard learns what the second touch is for.
24. **The Marked Calendar**: A coworker circles the 14th of every month but never explains; an office friend's gentle curiosity reveals a promise being kept.
25. **The Closed Register**: A grocery store never opens lane four though it works fine; a new employee learns the reason and stops asking to.
26. **The Two Tickets**: A man buys two movie tickets every Friday and watches alone; the usher's quiet observation uncovers the love behind it.
27. **The Repainted Mailbox**: A teen notices the same mailbox number repainted weekly; following the trail reveals a father's hope.
28. **The Humming Fridge Note**: A tenant tapes a new handwritten note to the building fridge each week, signed only with a heart; residents finally discover who.
29. **The Sunday Drive to Nowhere**: An old couple drives the same empty loop every Sunday; their grandson rides along once and understands.
30. **The Kept Streetlight**: A man pays the city to keep one specific streetlight on; a reporter's question reveals what happened beneath it.


## E03 — Hidden Burden (empathy reframe)

*Driver: empathy. Someone who reads as cold, rigid, or selfish is privately carrying a heavy weight — judgment dissolves into respect.*

1. **The Five O'Clock Nurse**: Coworkers resent the nurse who leaves exactly at 5 and never covers shifts — until one follows her to the hospice where she sits with her dying son.
2. **The Quiet Coworker**: An office mocks a man who never joins happy hour; a colleague learns he rushes home to care for a wife with early-onset dementia.
3. **The Strict Teacher**: Students call her heartless for never accepting late work — until one discovers she stays past midnight grading because she has no one to go home to and these kids are her life.
4. **The Distant Dad**: A teen resents his father for always working; the boy finds the second job's pay stubs covering his late mother's medical debt.
5. **The Cheap Tipper**: A regular is branded a stingy tipper until a server learns he's quietly paying every dishwasher's rent that month.
6. **The Manager Who Never Smiles**: Staff fear the grim shift manager; a new hire finds her crying in her car over the foster kids she's fighting to keep.
7. **The Neighbor Who Won't Wave**: A street thinks the old man is rude for ignoring everyone; a kid learns he's going deaf and ashamed to admit it.
8. **The Absent Friend**: A woman is called flaky for canceling every plan; her best friend discovers she's been donating plasma twice a week to afford insulin.
9. **The Cold Landlord**: Tenants despise the landlord who never chats; one learns he eats one meal a day to keep their rent frozen after his own losses.
10. **The Sleeping Janitor**: Students laugh at the janitor who naps in the break room; a teacher learns he works two jobs and sleeps three hours to raise grandkids.
11. **The Mom Who's Always Late**: Other parents judge her for missing every game; her daughter learns she's been visiting the prison to see her father, ashamed to say.
12. **The Silent Vet**: Regulars think the diner's old veteran is unfriendly; a waitress realizes loud rooms send him back to a war he can't escape.
13. **The Penny-Pincher**: A man is mocked for reusing tea bags and clipping coupons; his niece finds he's saved every cent for her surgery.
14. **The Unsmiling Receptionist**: Patients complain she's curt; a doctor learns she's working through chemo and refusing to take a single day off.
15. **The Dad Who Skips the Recital**: A girl thinks her father doesn't care; he's outside in the lot, unable to climb stairs since the stroke he hid from her.
16. **The Coworker Who Hoards Food**: An office judges a man for taking extra from every catered lunch; a colleague follows him to the shelter where his family sleeps.
17. **The Grumpy Crossing Guard**: Kids call her mean for never chatting; a parent learns she lip-reads traffic because she lost her hearing keeping children safe.
18. **The Teacher Who Won't Retire**: Faculty whisper she's clinging to the job; the principal learns her pension was stolen and the classroom is all she has left.
19. **The Roommate Who's Never Home**: A man resents his absent roommate; he discovers the guy sleeps at the hospital beside his comatose brother.
20. **The Boss Who Eats Alone**: Employees think the owner is aloof; an intern learns he gave up his own salary for a year to make payroll.
21. **The Woman Who Won't Donate**: Coworkers judge her for skipping every charity drive; one learns every dollar already goes to her sister's three kids.
22. **The Quiet Bus Driver**: Riders call him rude for never speaking; a passenger realizes he's mute and memorized every regular's stop out of care.
23. **The Father Who Misses Holidays**: His kids resent his absence each Thanksgiving; they learn he volunteers at the morgue so colleagues with families can be home.
24. **The Cold Surgeon**: Nurses find him robotic; one sees him break down alone after losing a patient who looked like his late daughter.
25. **The Neighbor Who Never Lends**: A block thinks she's selfish; a girl learns she's rationing everything to keep her unemployed son's family fed.
26. **The Man Who Won't Sit**: Bus riders judge him for not taking the open seat for years; a nurse recognizes the back brace under his coat.
27. **The Aunt Who Forgets Birthdays**: A niece feels unloved; she finds a drawer of unsent, unaffordable gifts her aunt couldn't bear to give empty-handed.
28. **The Coach Who Benches the Star**: Parents rage at him; they learn he's protecting the boy's untreated heart condition the family hid.
29. **The Tenant Who Plays Music Late**: Neighbors complain nightly; one learns it's the only thing that calms his autistic daughter to sleep.
30. **The Waitress Who Rushes Everyone**: Customers find her brusque; a regular learns she's racing between three jobs to bury her mother with dignity.


## E04 — The Long Wait / Devotion

*Driver: loyalty, longing. A solitary ritual tied to a place or person, sustained for years — its meaning revealed. No antagonist.*

1. **The Repainted Bench**: For 31 years a man repaints the same park bench each spring — it's where he proposed, and where she promised to come back.
2. **The Pier at Dawn**: An old woman walks to the end of the pier every morning at 6:10 — the minute her fisherman husband's boat was due home forty years ago.
3. **The Set Table**: A widower lays two plates every dinner; the second is for the wife he still talks to across the table each night.
4. **The Airport Greeter**: A man holds a handmade sign at arrivals every Sunday for a daughter who stopped flying home a decade ago — hoping one Sunday she'll appear.
5. **The Porch Light**: A mother keeps the porch light on every night since her son shipped out; she'll keep it burning until he walks back up the steps.
6. **The Unmailed Letters**: A man writes his missing wife a letter every Friday and files it in a box, certain she'll read them all someday.
7. **The Same Diner Booth**: Every anniversary a man orders for two at the booth where he met her, sliding the second cup across the table.
8. **The Garden She Planted**: A husband tends his late wife's roses exactly as she did, refusing to let a single bloom fail in her absence.
9. **The Frozen Clock**: A woman keeps the kitchen clock stopped at 4:17 — the moment her daughter was born and, years later, the moment she'll always wait to relive.
10. **The Weekly Drive**: An old man drives to the coast every Saturday because his wife loved the water; he describes the waves aloud since she can no longer see them.
11. **The Saved Seat at Church**: A man leaves the aisle seat empty every service for the brother who promised to find faith and come sit beside him.
12. **The Lighthouse Visit**: A retired keeper climbs the decommissioned lighthouse each storm out of a vow to a ship that never came in.
13. **The Birthday Cake for One**: A father bakes his estranged son's favorite cake every birthday, lighting candles for a chair that's been empty eleven years.
14. **The Train Platform Wave**: A woman waves at the noon train daily — her husband worked that line, and waving is how she still says hello.
15. **The Kept Workshop**: A widow keeps her husband's workshop dusted and ready, tools laid out as if he'll come finish the cradle he started.
16. **The Annual Ticket**: A man buys two tickets to the team's opener every year and sits beside the empty seat his father used to fill.
17. **The Shoreline Search**: An elderly woman walks the beach each tide collecting sea glass for the granddaughter she's sure will visit again.
18. **The Unchanged Room**: Parents leave their soldier's room untouched for decades, the bed turned down, waiting for a homecoming that defines their days.
19. **The Coffee at the Counter**: A man orders his late friend's usual every morning and lets it go cold — a standing toast to a promise they made as boys.
20. **The Dance at Closing**: A widower returns to the ballroom every Friday and dances one slow song alone to the song that was theirs.
21. **The Window Watch**: An old woman sits at the same window every afternoon watching for the mail carrier who once brought a letter that changed her life.
22. **The Mended Quilt**: A husband re-stitches his wife's unfinished quilt one square a year, refusing to ever call it done.
23. **The Phone That Still Rings**: A man keeps the old landline connected for the daughter who once called every Sunday, just in case she dials again.
24. **The Empty Swing**: A grandfather oils the backyard swing each season for grandchildren who've grown and gone, keeping it ready for their return.
25. **The Same Park Loop**: A woman walks her late dog's exact route every evening, pausing at his favorite tree to wait a moment longer.
26. **The Reserved Hotel Room**: A man books the same honeymoon suite every anniversary and toasts the balcony where she said yes.
27. **The Folded Flag Vigil**: A mother polishes her son's case and folded flag every morning, speaking the day's news to him as she works.
28. **The Open Gate**: A farmer leaves the east gate unlatched every night for the runaway brother he believes will one day walk the road home.
29. **The Sunday Hymn**: A man hums his mother's favorite hymn at her grave each Sunday, finishing the verse she never got to.
30. **The Lit Candle in the Window**: A woman places a fresh candle in the window every dusk for the fishing crew lost at sea, so they'll always find the shore.


## E05 — Comeuppance (the downfall)

*Driver: catharsis. The wrongdoer's own behavior brings about their fall. The antagonist is the focus.*

1. **The Bragging Landlord**: A landlord posts a smug video evicting a family on Christmas Eve — the clip reaches the loan officer reviewing his own overdue foreclosure.
2. **The Viral Manager**: A manager films himself firing a worker "to set an example"; the internet identifies him as the one who faked his degree to get hired.
3. **The Parking Spot Tyrant**: A man keys a car blocking "his" spot on camera, not realizing the dashcam is rolling — and the car belongs to the HOA attorney.
4. **The Cruel Customer**: A diner berates a server and demands she be fired; the owner plays the lobby audio at the next town meeting where the customer is running for office.
5. **The Gloating Executive**: A VP mocks a "useless" department in a company-wide email he meant to send privately — to the board that just made that department the new strategy.
6. **The Line-Cutter**: A man shoves past an elderly woman at the airport, films his "win," and posts it; the airline bans him as the clip trends.
7. **The Fake Heir**: A nephew forges a will and flaunts his inheritance; the funeral home reveals the deceased recorded a video naming the real beneficiary.
8. **The Smug Cheater**: A contractor brags about overcharging seniors; one "senior" he scammed is a retired fraud investigator wearing a wire.
9. **The Boastful Bully**: A coach humiliates a benchwarmer for the crowd; the kid's recruiting video of the moment ends the coach's career, not the kid's.
10. **The Influencer's Stunt**: An influencer fakes giving money to a homeless man then takes it back on camera; the backlash erases her brand deals overnight.
11. **The Rigged Contest**: A bakery owner rigs a town contest against a widow; the judges, tipped off, award her live as he's exposed.
12. **The Power-Tripping Guard**: A security guard bars a "shabby" man from a gala for fun; the man is the keynote, and the guard's manager saw it all.
13. **The Tax-Dodge Brag**: A businessman boasts at a party about hiding income; a guest is the IRS auditor he'd been avoiding.
14. **The Stolen Idea**: A boss presents an intern's plan as his own to applause; the intern's timestamped email is already in the CEO's inbox.
15. **The Public Shamer**: A woman posts a stranger's photo mocking his clothes; he turns out to be the recruiter for the job she just applied to.
16. **The Greedy Flipper**: A house-flipper lies about a foundation crack to a young couple; the inspector he bribed flips and reports him.
17. **The Country Club Snob**: A member humiliates a caddie in front of guests; the caddie's quiet recording costs the member his board seat.
18. **The Fake Charity Gala**: An organizer skims a children's fund and brags about the yacht; a volunteer accountant has every receipt.
19. **The Road-Rage Driver**: A man tailgates and brake-checks a "slowpoke," filming his rant; the slowpoke is an off-duty officer and the video is evidence.
20. **The Demeaning Professor**: A professor ridicules a returning-student mom's essay aloud; she withdraws, publishes it, and it wins the award he chairs.
21. **The Nepotist's Speech**: A CEO's son mocks the "lifers" at a banquet; the lifers own the vested shares that vote him out that quarter.
22. **The Catfish**: A man builds a fake profile to humiliate a coworker; the screenshots loop back to HR with his own IP attached.
23. **The Wedding Crasher Lie**: A woman lies that the caterer ruined the wedding to avoid paying; the venue's cameras show her tampering with the food.
24. **The Slumlord's Inspection**: A landlord bribes past a code check and brags online; the post becomes the city's case file against him.
25. **The Mocking Heir**: A daughter belittles the housekeeper at the will reading; the will leaves everything to the housekeeper, with a note explaining why.
26. **The Faked Injury**: A man fakes a fall to sue a small shop; the shop's new camera caught him rehearsing it in the parking lot.
27. **The Cruel Review Bomber**: A rival floods a café with fake one-stars; the platform traces every review to his single office IP.
28. **The Arrogant Surgeon**: A surgeon humiliates a nurse who flags an error; the error he ignored becomes the malpractice case that ends him, vindicating her.
29. **The Ticket Scalper's Brag**: A scalper gloats about gouging fans of a benefit show; the artist cancels his order and names him from the stage.
30. **The Boss Who Skipped Payroll**: An owner buys a sports car the week he "can't afford" wages; the photo he posts becomes the labor board's exhibit A.


## E06 — Bittersweet / Too Late

*Driver: tears. The truth arrives after the window has closed. No happy reversal, no antagonist consequence — grief and love.*

1. **The Unopened Voicemail**: A son finally listens to his father's last voicemail two weeks after the funeral — "Check the box under the stairs. I kept every letter you sent."
2. **The Late Visit**: A daughter drives home to finally forgive her mother, arriving to a quiet house and a neighbor who says she passed that morning.
3. **The Reservation for Two**: A man arrives to surprise his wife at their anniversary table, not knowing the hospital called an hour ago — he sits with the second menu and waits.
4. **The Reconciliation Letter**: An estranged brother mails an apology after thirty years; it's returned unopened, stamped "deceased," the week it would have healed everything.
5. **The Birthday Card**: A father buys his daughter's birthday card early for once; he dies before the date, and she finds it signed, waiting in his coat.
6. **The Last Dance Plan**: A husband schedules dance lessons to surprise his wife for their 50th; the instructor arrives to a widower who still wants the first lesson, alone.
7. **The Saved Seat**: A mother keeps a graduation seat for the son deployed overseas; the dress uniform officers reach her just as the names begin.
8. **The Returned Ring**: A man finally affords the ring he couldn't years ago and flies to propose again; her sister meets him at the gate with the news.
9. **The Finished Painting**: An artist completes the portrait of her grandmother to surprise her; she carries it to the nursing home the day after the bed is emptied.
10. **The Forgiven Debt**: A landlord drives over to tell a struggling tenant the rent is forgiven; the ambulance is already pulling away.
11. **The Reunion Flight**: A daughter books a surprise flight to reconcile with her dad; turbulence delays her, and she lands to a missed-call screen full of his number.
12. **The Last Recipe**: A son finally asks his mother to teach him her bread; she writes the first line of the recipe before the stroke takes the rest.
13. **The Garden Bench**: A man builds the garden bench his wife always wanted; he sets it the morning of the day he becomes the one who sits there alone.
14. **The Phone Call Home**: A soldier finally calls to say he's coming home for good; the line that picks up is a cousin explaining the accident from that morning.
15. **The Old Friend's Show**: A woman buys tickets to surprise the bandmate she abandoned years ago; the marquee reads "In Memoriam" when she arrives.
16. **The Adoption Records**: A man finally opens his sealed file to find his birth mother; the address leads to a fresh headstone and a neighbor who says she searched for him too.
17. **The Apology Speech**: A daughter rehearses an apology for her father's retirement party; she delivers it instead as a eulogy she never meant to give.
18. **The Last Catch**: A son clears his schedule to finally go fishing with his dad; he arrives at the lake with two rods and a folded note that says "soon, son — soon."
19. **The Unworn Dress**: A mother sews her daughter's wedding dress in secret; the daughter finds it finished in the closet the week after losing her.
20. **The Returned Soldier's Dog**: A family keeps a deployed son's dog faithfully; the dog greets the flag-draped case at the door it watched every night.
21. **The Reconnected Sisters**: Two estranged sisters finally agree to meet for coffee; one waits at the table reading the text that the other won't be coming, ever.
22. **The Final Lesson**: A grandfather promises to teach his grandson to drive at sixteen; the boy passes his test alone and parks outside the hospice that just went quiet.
23. **The Late Promotion**: A company finally honors a lifer's decades of work; the plaque is unveiled at the memorial his coworkers throw instead.
24. **The Saved Money**: A man saves for years for the trip his wife dreamed of; he scatters her ashes at the destination they never reached together.
25. **The Mended Friendship**: A woman drives across the state to repair a friendship she broke; she's handed the journal where her friend wrote she'd hoped she would.
26. **The Last Game of Catch**: A father recovers enough to finally play catch; he steps outside with the glove to find his son grown and gone, the yard empty.
27. **The Unanswered Door**: A daughter brings groceries to apologize to her father; the spare key lets her into a tidy house and a chair still warm with absence.
28. **The Late Diagnosis Gift**: A husband plans the cruise the day his wife's tests come back; they spend her last good week choosing a trip they'll never take.
29. **The Returned Letter from War**: A widow finally receives her husband's last letter, delayed sixty years — written the morning before he was lost.
30. **The Forgotten Birthday Remembered**: A man wakes early to finally celebrate his mother's birthday after years of forgetting; the calendar reminder pings beside the sympathy cards.


## E07 — Test / Secret Evaluator

*Driver: suspense about a choice. A setup quietly tests character; the evaluator reveals themselves and the choice carries consequences.*

1. **The Dropped Wallet**: A fat wallet is "lost" outside three job candidates' interviews; the one who turns it in unaware learns the CEO was watching the lobby camera.
2. **The Stranded Stranger**: A franchise owner poses as a broke traveler at his own stores to see which manager helps and which turns him away.
3. **The Disabled Applicant**: A founder applies to his own company in a wheelchair to see who holds the door — and who steps over him.
4. **The Crying Child in the Aisle**: A retail chain's owner plants a lost child to see which clerk kneels down and which calls it someone else's problem.
5. **The Short-Changed Customer**: A diner owner has a "confused" elder overpay to learn which server quietly returns the difference.
6. **The Rude Old Man**: A board chair plays a cranky nobody at the gala to test how the staff treat someone who can do nothing for them.
7. **The Final Interview Lunch**: A candidate thinks the interview's over; the real test is how he treats the waiter — and the CEO is watching.
8. **The Homeless Veteran Outside HQ**: A company head sits by his own entrance in rags to see which employee shares a coffee and which looks away.
9. **The Misdelivered Package**: A valuable parcel lands on the wrong porch as a test; the neighbor's honesty decides a quiet inheritance.
10. **The Lottery Ticket Test**: An old woman hands cashiers a "winning" ticket to see who tells her the truth about its value.
11. **The Broken-Down Car**: A scholarship donor stages a roadside breakdown near campus to find the student who stops without knowing who he is.
12. **The Overflowing Tip Jar**: A café owner overfills the tip jar and steps away to see which barista pockets it and which guards it.
13. **The Lost Tourist**: A hotel chain's heiress plays a confused foreigner to test which concierge is patient and which is dismissive.
14. **The Extra Change**: A grocery magnate hands cashiers too much money to see who chases him down the lot to return it.
15. **The Sick Passenger**: An airline executive fakes illness in economy to learn which flight attendant truly cares past the script.
16. **The Forgotten Bag on the Bus**: A transit director leaves a bag of cash to find the driver who turns it in untouched.
17. **The Mock Layoff**: A new owner tells staff the company may close to see who rallies to help and who loots the supply closet.
18. **The Babysitter's Hidden Camera**: Parents test a sitter not on chores but on kindness; the nanny-cam reveals who she is when no one's "watching."
19. **The Beggar at the Wedding**: A bride's grandmother sends a "beggar" to the reception to see which guest shows grace before the inheritance is decided.
